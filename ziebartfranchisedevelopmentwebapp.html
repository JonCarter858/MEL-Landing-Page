Persona Diary Insights

Authentic inner dialogue revealing emotional triggers and decision drivers

Taylor Nguyen - Thursday Evening

Home Office - Phoenix, AZ
Another reorg announcement today. Third one in two years. My spreadsheet shows I've saved enough for a down payment on... something. But what? Every franchise website feels like a carnival barker. Every indie path feels like reinventing the wheel. I keep driving past that ceramic coating shop on Camelback—always busy, always cars waiting. There's something there. People protecting what they own. Simple. Repeatable. Not sexy, but steady. I need a model with training wheels, not hero worship. Tomorrow I'm blocking two hours to map out non-negotiables. No more circling. Time to test a thesis with real data, real reference calls. If I can run logistics for 500 SKUs, I can run a shop with 5 service types. The question isn't can I—it's which system gives me the best odds.

Miguel Alvarez - Saturday Morning

Shop Floor - Albuquerque, NM
6 AM and I'm already here, mixing compounds. Love the craft, hate the chaos. Lost another tech to that franchise down on Montgomery—better pay, actual training path, benefits. My Yelp reviews beat theirs, but their parking lot stays fuller. It's the warranty cards. The national ads. The fleet accounts I can't crack. I'm tired of being David without a slingshot. Spent last night comparing franchise FDDs. Most are just logos and invoices. But a few... actual field support, vendor deals that would cut my costs 20%, marketing that doesn't drain my account for "testing." My wife asked what I want for our anniversary. I want Tuesday afternoons off. I want a system that runs when I'm not here. I want my name on something bigger than my name.

Jordan Patel - Sunday Evening

Home Study - Boise, ID
Three FDDs spread across my desk. Ziebart, Competitor A, Competitor B. My CPA flagged the differences in Item 19. The attorneys marked territory language. But what I really need to know: who answers the phone at 7 PM when something breaks? Which ops manual is actually updated? Whose franchisees renew at 90%+ rates? The automotive protection space makes sense—recession-resistant, recurring revenue potential, growing vehicle ages. Ziebart has the history, but history doesn't equal execution. Tomorrow's calls: three multi-unit operators, two who converted from competitors, one who built from scratch. I need to hear exhaustion levels, not earnings claims. I need to know about Sunday mornings, not just Saturday nights. Territory 1432 could anchor the cluster. Time to stop analyzing and start interrogating.

Casey Brooks - Monday Morning

Lot Office - Salt Lake City, UT
Contract's on my desk. Territory's confirmed. Financing approved. But I keep running the launch sequence in my head. Site selection—I've got three options, need boots on the ground this week. Hiring—my GM prospect from Denver is wavering, need backup options. Equipment lead times are 8 weeks; that puts us into February. Pre-opening marketing should start 45 days out. My reconditioning manager wants in as a minority partner—could solve the expertise gap. The only thing worse than not starting is starting wrong. I've built businesses before, but never with someone else's brand on the door. The support better be real. The playbook better be current. The field team better answer their phones. Time to stop planning and start building. Signing Wednesday. Grand opening in Q2. No looking back.

Riley Carter - Wednesday Night

Home Garage - Denver, CO
9 PM and I'm detailing my Tacoma. Again. It's meditation, I guess. Tomorrow's another pipeline review where my "commits" might flip to "maybes" because procurement got cold feet. Six figures feels like treading water when it all hangs on some VP's mood. My neighbor just sold his HVAC business for 4x revenue. Four years, built it from nothing. Meanwhile, I'm building someone else's ARR. There's got to be something between corporate ladder and startup lottery. Something boring. Something that people need whether the economy's up or down. Something where the work you put in stays yours. I watch these YouTube channels about ceramic coating—oddly satisfying. Wonder what those shops actually make? Probably more stable than my commission swings. Maybe it's time to stop wondering and start researching. Real businesses. Real assets. Real equity.

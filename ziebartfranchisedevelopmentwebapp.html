Diary Insights

Authentic inner dialogue of each persona

Taylor Nguyen

Thursday Evening - Home Office - Phoenix, AZ
Another reorg announcement today. Third one in two years. My spreadsheet shows I've saved enough for a down payment on... something. But what? Every franchise website feels like a carnival barker. Every indie path feels like reinventing the wheel. I keep driving past that ceramic coating shop on Camelback—always busy, always cars waiting. There's something there. People protecting what they own. Simple. Repeatable. Not sexy, but steady. I need a model with training wheels, not hero worship. Tomorrow I'm blocking two hours to map out non-negotiables. No more circling. Time to test a thesis with real data, real reference calls.

Miguel Alvarez

Saturday Morning - Shop Floor - Albuquerque, NM
6 AM and I'm already here, mixing compounds. Love the craft, hate the chaos. Lost another tech to that franchise down on Montgomery—better pay, actual training path, benefits. My Yelp reviews beat theirs, but their parking lot stays fuller. It's the warranty cards. The national ads. The fleet accounts I can't crack. I'm tired of being David without a slingshot. Spent last night comparing franchise FDDs. Most are just logos and invoices. But a few... actual field support, vendor deals that would cut my costs 20%, marketing that doesn't drain my account for "testing."

Jordan Patel

Sunday Evening - Home Study - Boise, ID
Three FDDs spread across my desk. Ziebart, Competitor A, Competitor B. My CPA flagged the differences in Item 19. The attorneys marked territory language. But what I really need to know: who answers the phone at 7 PM when something breaks? Which ops manual is actually updated? Whose franchisees renew at 90%+ rates? The automotive protection space makes sense—recession-resistant, recurring revenue potential, growing vehicle ages. Tomorrow's calls: three multi-unit operators, two who converted from competitors, one who built from scratch.
